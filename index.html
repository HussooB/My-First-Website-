Things about Butterflies

    
  Butterfly Wings Are Transparent:Although we see them as colorful creatures, 
    the wings of butterflies are actually made up of layers of chitin,
    a protein that forms an insects exoskeleton, and are transparent. 
    The colors we see are due to the reflection of light 
    from thousands of tiny scales covering their wings

  Taste With Their Feet:Butterflies have taste receptors on their feet,
    which they use to find host plants and food.
     They can taste by stepping on their food sources, like fermenting fruit

  All-Liquid Diet:Adult butterflies can only feed on liquids, primarily nectar.
     They have a proboscis, similar to a drinking straw, which they use to sip their meals 

  Proboscis Assembly:Upon emerging from the chrysalis, 
     a butterflys first task is to assemble its proboscis 
     from two separate parts into one functioning feeding tube

  Puddling for Minerals:Butterflies sometimes drink from mud puddles to obtain minerals and salts, 
     a behavior more common in males

  Group Names:A group of butterflies is called a flutter, flight, or kaleidoscope
      when in flight, and a roost when resting. Caterpillars in groups are known as an army

  Four Wings:Butterflies actually have four wings, not twoa pair of forewings
      and a pair of hind wings

  Compound Eyes:They have up to 17,000 mini eyes within each compound eye,
      allowing them to see a range of colors including ultraviolet light

  Flight Speed:Some butterflies, like skippers, can fly at speeds of up to 37 MPH

  Diverse Species:There are approximately 20,000 species of butterflies found across the globe
